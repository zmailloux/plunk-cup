# AI-Suggested Rule Changes (2026)

> ⚠ **These are AI-generated proposals, not official rules.** Generated from analysis of the
> 2025 season (final rosters, draft grades, trades, tanking patterns). Nothing here is in
> effect — they're conversation starters for a league vote.

Ranked by impact. Each has the reasoning and the main trade-off.

## 1. Weekly median game
Each week, also play the **league median score** for a second W/L.
- **Why:** cuts schedule luck. This year the best-drafted roster (Devin, A+) finished 4th while
  below-par drafters rode favorable matchups to the podium. A median game rewards good teams
  regardless of who they drew, and keeps eliminated teams sweating every week.
- **Trade-off:** doubles the number of "games," so records look different (more wins/losses).

## 2. Draft lottery for the fixed Rounds 1–4
Keep worst-teams-pick-first, but make it a **weighted lottery** (NBA-style) instead of guaranteed order.
- **Why:** with reverse-order fixed picks that can't be traded, **tanking is directly rewarded** —
  exactly what happened in 2025 (a team sold vets, finished 7th, banked premium picks). A lottery
  keeps strugglers likely to pick early without guaranteeing the top pick for losing on purpose.
- **Trade-off:** adds a lottery step; a genuinely bad team could slip a couple spots.

## 3. FAB allowed in trades (+ open R3+ picks, keep the R1–2 ban)
Let managers include **FAB $** and **round 3+ future picks** in trades. Keep the ban on trading R1–2 picks.
- **Why:** more trade currency = more deals = more fun. Keeping the R1–2 ban stops a tanker from
  flipping the reverse-order pick reward for win-now vets and double-dipping.
- **Trade-off:** FAB accounting to track; commissioners verify.

## 4. Trim the bench (5 → 3 or 4)
Reduce bench slots.
- **Why:** deep benches let strong teams hoard talent (Devin's A+ roster stranded points on the
  bench; lots of drafted players churned unused). Shallower benches force real cuts → more talent
  on waivers → more parity and more FAB bidding action.
- **Trade-off:** more brutal bye-week/injury management; fewer stash spots.

## 5. Steeper undrafted-keeper cost (R7 → R5)
Raise the keeper cost for waiver/undrafted players.
- **Why:** R7 is too cheap — this year that made **Matthew Stafford (335 pts)** and **Trevor
  Lawrence (316 pts)** keepable for a 7th. A waiver hit shouldn't cost less than a solid mid-round pick.
- **Trade-off:** discourages keeping waiver gems; may reduce in-season waiver excitement slightly.

## 6. Weekly high-score bonus + toilet-bowl stakes
Give the week's top scorer a small reward (an extra win or +FAB), and add real stakes to the
last-place bracket (a punishment tradition, maybe a minor draft perk).
- **Why:** gives dead/tanking teams something to play for so nobody checks out.
- **Trade-off:** more moving parts to administer.

---

*If you do just three: **median game + draft lottery + FAB-in-trades.** Cheap to adopt, hits
fairness, anti-tank, and deal-making without changing the league's identity.*
