# Plunk Cup — 2025 Draft Report Card

*How well did each manager actually draft? Graded on season-long fantasy production
vs. where each player was taken.*

---

## Methodology

- **Source data:** 2025 draft results (round, overall pick, position) + season-end
  Fan Pts pulled from final rosters (league custom scoring).
- **Credit follows the pick, not the trade.** A player's season points count for whoever
  **drafted** him, even if traded away. So a team that drafted well then sold assets still
  earns full draft credit (see *Tanking* below).
- **Round Par:** the average Fan Pts of all players drafted in a given round. A pick's
  **delta** = player points − round par. Positive = value; negative = reach/bust.
- **Positional finish** (e.g., "RB4", "QB9") is computed among all players we have points
  for, and used as qualitative color.
- **Team grade** = average delta across that team's picks with known points.
- **Keeper lens.** Per league rules, **keeper eligibility is round 5 and onward** — players
  drafted R1–R4 **cannot be kept**. Keeper cost = the player's original draft round, getting
  1 round more expensive each year; undrafted (waiver) players keep at a R7 pick (then R6);
  2 keepers max, 3-year lifespan. So a late-round steal is worth *more* than its points
  suggest — you can hold it cheap. See **Keeper Watch** below.

### ⚠ Limitations — read before arguing
1. **UNK picks:** some drafted players were dropped/churned and never landed on a tracked
   final roster, so we have **no season points** for them (Mahomes, Mark Andrews, Sam LaPorta,
   CJ Stroud, etc.). These are excluded from a team's average — teams with many UNKs have
   noisier grades. UNK counts listed per team.
2. **Round par mixes positions.** RBs dominated early-round scoring, so a solid early WR/QB
   can read "below par" against an RB-heavy round. Treat R1–R3 non-RB deltas with a grain of salt.
3. Custom league scoring — not standard PPR. Points are league-specific.

## 🏳️ Tanking & Sell-Off Analysis

Draft grades credit the *pick*, but the trade log shows who cashed out. Teams that dealt
productive drafted players for **future picks / next-year assets**:

- **Zach (Drunk Drafting)** — drafted De'Von Achane (R3, 321.8 pts) and Drake London
  (R2, 201.9) and **sold both** (Achane+Love → Kervin; London → Jared). Also won the
  **manual pick swap**, turning his R17 into Jared's R4 for the 2026 draft. Classic
  sell-the-present-buy-the-future rebuild. His D+ board understates the strategy — he
  correctly identified Achane as a gem, then flipped him.
- **Lucas (The RESET)** — dealt his drafted MHJ / Davante Adams / J.Warren to Jared. But
  Lucas was *retooling for a title run*, not tanking — and it worked (2nd).
- **Buyers:** Kervin (bought Achane + Love), Jared (bought MHJ/Adams/Warren), Paul
  (most active — Hunter, McMillan, Dobbins in and out).

**Takeaway:** The pure draft board (Devin ≥ Griffin ≥ Jared) and the final standings
(Griffin, Lucas, Paul on the podium) only partly overlap. Griffin is the one manager who
both drafted great **and** finished on top. Devin is the cautionary tale: an A+ board that
went 4th.

## 🔒 Keeper Watch (R5+ eligibility)

Keepers must have been drafted **round 5 or later** (or be undrafted/waiver = R7 cost).
R1–R4 picks are locked out. 2026 keeper cost = one round earlier than draft round (UDFA = R7).
Full per-player eligibility + cost is annotated in each `final roster/*.md` file.

**The cruel truth:** almost every team's *best* player is locked out at R1–R4 —

and **Paul** have their top scorer keepable (both hit at R5: Cook, McBride).

keeper core — Caleb Williams (R7 UDFA), RJ Harvey (R8), TreVeyon Henderson (R8), Omarion
Hampton (R5) — exactly what the sell-off was for. **Griffin** (Stafford R7 UDFA) and
**Lucas** (Lawrence R7 UDFA) landed elite QBs off waivers = the best keeper bargains in the league.

> Cost interpretation used: *drafted round − 1 for 2026*. If the commish meant "same round
> the first year," every drafted cost shifts back one round — flagged in each roster file.
